Production

My first taste of 'music production' was in 2016, when I started making mashups using Audacity and posting them on SoundCloud. One in particular (external), a mashup of Childish Gambino's 3005 and Feel Good Inc. by the Gorillaz, ended up gaining a reasonable amount of internet traction.

In around 2018, I started doing 'real' music production, making mostly hip-hop beats (that really don't sound very good). I now produce music using Ableton Live. Most of what I produce ends up gathering dust on my hard drive, but occasionally I end up making stuff that I think is worth showing the world.

This is a breakcore-ish track, and the favourite track I've produced. I think I could improve the mix on the low-end a bit though.


This is a short experiment I made to try out Synplant (external). There's lots of room for more improvement/layers here, but I loved the process of making the sounds.


This is an ambient track I made by partial accident, trying to record my performance of Debussy's Arabesque No. 1 from my electric piano, and getting some weird chopping issues in Ableton. I thought it sounded really cool, so I added some effects and voila! This is one of my only tracks to have a name: 'Market Square'.


I also occasionally update my SoundCloud (external) with new tracks, and is the first place I usually release them (if they get released).
